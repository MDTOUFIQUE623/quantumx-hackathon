In plain English: We help people trust elections online: only real voters get a pass, each person votes once, ballots stay secret but can be audited, and the math is chosen so future quantum computers cannot easily break old locks years later.

1 — Problem

Democracies depend on elections people can trust. Digital voting must be private, verifiable, and resistant to fraud — including threats that do not exist yet.

  • Wrong-area voting — voting outside one’s constituency
  • Double voting — same person votes twice
  • Ballot tampering — changing votes in transit
  • “Steal now, read later” — copy encrypted votes today; break old locks with tomorrow’s quantum computers
Example: A student uses a Delhi college ID to vote in a Pune-only election, or someone votes online and again by post — our demo blocks both. A spy archiving today’s vote files is like saving locked safes until a better drill exists; we use safes quantum drills are not expected to open.

3 — Who does what (4 roles)

RoleTechnical nameEveryday job

Example flow: Ananya registers in Pune → votes for a candidate → gets receipt #ABC → anyone sees #ABC on the board (not her name) → officer runs tally → “Candidate X: 42 votes.”
